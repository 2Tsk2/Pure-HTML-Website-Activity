An adjective is a word that describes a noun or pronoun.

Adjectives are words that describe the qualities or states of being of nouns: enormous, doglike, silly, yellow, fun, fast. They can also describe the quantity of nouns: many, few, millions, eleven.

>ðŸ“™ Degree of comparison

Adjectives come in three forms: absolute, comparative, and superlative. Absolute adjectives describe something in its own right.

Example: A cool guy
Example: A messy desk
Example: A mischievous cat

Comparative adjectives, unsurprisingly, make a comparison between two or more things.

Example: My brother is cooler than me.
Example: My desk is messier than yours.
Example: My cat is more mischievous than yours.

Superlative adjectives indicate that something has the highest degree of the quality in question.

Example: My brother is the coolest guy I know.
Example: My desk is the messiest desk in the office.
Example: My cat is the most mischievous cat in the neighborhood.
