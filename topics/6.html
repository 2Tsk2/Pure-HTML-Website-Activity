>ðŸ“—Direct speech

Direct speech repeats, or quotes, the exact words spoken. When we use direct speech in writing, we place the words spoken between quotation marks (" ") and there is no change in these words.

Example: She says, "What time will you be home?"
Example: She said, "What time will you be home?" and I said, "I don't know! "
Example: "There's a fly in my soup!" screamed Simone.
Example: John said, "There's an elephant outside the window."

>ðŸ“• Indirect speech

Reported or indirect speech is usually used to talk about the past, so we normally change the tense of the words spoken. We use reporting verbs like 'say', 'tell', 'ask', and we may use the word 'that' to introduce the reported words.

Example: She told him she was happy.
Example: She said that she had seen him.
Example: He told me that he was tired.
Example: She was speaking on the telephone.

>ðŸ“—Tense changes when using reported speech

Normally, the tense in reported speech is one tense back in time from the tense in direct speech:

Example: "I always drink coffee", she said --> She said that she always drank coffee.
Example: "Bill arrived on Saturday", he said. --> He said that Bill had arrived on Saturday.
Example: "I am reading a book", he explained. --> He explained that he was reading a book
Example: "I have been to Spain", he told me. --> He told me that he had been to Spain.
